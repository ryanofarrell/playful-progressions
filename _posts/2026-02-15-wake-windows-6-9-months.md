The 6-9 month phase is an incredibly exciting time! Your baby is becoming so much more interactive and mobile. This is the stage where many babies start to sit independently, show signs of crawling, and develop more precise fine motor skills like the pincer grasp. Wake windows are stretching out, giving you more time to play and engage. Here are some OT-approved ways to make the most of this dynamic stage!

## 1. Encouraging Independent Sitting
As your baby’s core strength improves, they will start to sit up with less support. This is a great time to practice sitting on the floor.

* **Supportive Play:** Start by placing toys just out of reach while they are seated to encourage them to twist and reach, which strengthens their core and obliques. You can sit behind them to catch them if they lose their balance, or use a nursing pillow or cushions around them for a soft landing.
* **Avoid "Containers":** While it’s tempting to use seated activity centers, floor time is much better for developing the core muscles needed for sitting and eventually standing. Try to limit container time as much as possible.

## 2. Pre-Crawling and Crawling
Crawling is a huge milestone that requires coordination, strength, and bilateral integration (using both sides of the body together).

* **Tummy Time is Still Key:** Even at this age, tummy time is crucial. It’s the starting position for crawling.
* **The "Pivot":** Before crawling forward, many babies learn to pivot in circles on their tummies. Place toys in a circle around them to encourage this motion.
* **Getting on Hands and Knees:** Help your baby get into a quadruped (hands and knees) position. You can place a small towel or your hands under their belly to provide gentle support. Gently rock them back and forth; this vestibular input helps their brain map the movement!
* **Obstacle Courses:** Once they start moving (even if it's an army crawl), create mini obstacle courses with couch cushions or pillows for them to climb over.

## 3. Developing Fine Motor Skills (The Pincer Grasp)
Around this time, babies begin to refine how they pick things up, transitioning from a "raking" motion with their whole hand to using their thumb and index finger (the pincer grasp).

* **Messy Eating:** Introducing solids is a wonderful sensory and fine motor activity! Allow them to pick up small, safe pieces of food (like puffed cereal or soft, cooked veggies). It’s messy, but the practice is invaluable.
* **Pulling Activities:** Babies love pulling things out! Fill an empty tissue box or a whisk with small pieces of fabric or safe items and let them pull them out.
* **Cause and Effect Toys:** Toys with buttons to push, dials to turn, or flaps to lift are fantastic for fine motor control and teaching cause and effect.

## 4. Object Permanence Games
Your baby is starting to understand that things still exist even when they can’t see them. This cognitive leap is fun to explore!

* **Peek-a-Boo:** The classic! It never gets old and is a perfect way to practice object permanence.
* **Hide and Seek with Toys:** Hide a favorite toy under a blanket or cup while they are watching and see if they will pull the blanket away to find it.

## 5. Sensory Exploration
Babies learn about the world through their senses. The 6-9 month range is a great time for more structured (but still safe) sensory play.

* **Water Play:** A shallow baking dish with a little water and some floating toys is a huge hit (always supervise closely!).
* **Sensory Bags:** Fill a ziplock bag with hair gel, a little water, and some small objects (like googly eyes or beads). Tape the edges securely to the floor or a highchair tray for them to squish and explore.
* **Texture Boards:** Tape different textures (bubble wrap, sandpaper, velvet, aluminum foil) to a piece of cardboard and let them run their hands over it.

Enjoy this interactive and busy phase! They are learning so much every single day.
