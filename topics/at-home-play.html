Compassionate, play-based education

You don’t need expensive toys to promote your child’s development! Some of the most powerful developmental and sensory exercises can be done in under five minutes using simple items you already have in your kitchen or closet—like aluminum foil, painter’s tape, muffin tins, and laundry baskets. Therapeutic play is about engaging your child's curiosity while gently building fine motor, gross motor, and regulation skills.

Pillars of Everyday Play

How quick, low-cost activities build massive motor, sensory, and cognitive skills.

Five Minute Fridays

A weekly series of quick, highly therapeutic play setups designed for busy families. Every activity takes less than five minutes to set up, costs virtually nothing, and utilizes household objects to achieve specific developmental goals (like bilateral coordination or core strength).

Household Staples

Learn how kitchen cabinet items hold secret developmental benefits! Peeling painter's tape off the wall builds pincer grasping; crumbling and flattening aluminum foil builds hand strength; pushing a weighted laundry basket provides soothing proprioceptive "heavy work."

OT Benefits of Simple Play

Simple play captures children's intrinsic motivation. Rather than drilling exercises, a child who is trying to rescue "trapped" toys inside a muffin tin is naturally practicing sorting, fine motor precision, wrist stability, bilateral integration, and cognitive problem-solving!
